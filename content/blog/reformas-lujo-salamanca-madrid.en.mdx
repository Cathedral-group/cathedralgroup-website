## Luxury renovations in Salamanca: where excellence is the standard

The [Barrio de Salamanca](/zonas/reformas-salamanca) is a byword for exclusivity in Madrid. Its period buildings from the late 19th and early 20th centuries — with high ceilings, original cornicing and generous layouts — are the perfect canvas for renovations of the highest order.

### What makes the Barrio de Salamanca unique

**Period architecture**

Salamanca's buildings are characterised by ceilings of 3 to 4 metres in height, original plaster cornicing, parquet floors with geometric patterns and façades of stone or exposed brick. A [luxury renovation](/servicios/reformas-integrales-madrid) should respect and enhance these features.

**Specific regulations**

As it sits within a heritage conservation area, renovations in Salamanca must comply with additional regulations governing the preservation of façades and communal features. This calls for an architect with experience in the neighbourhood.

**The client profile**

The typical owner in Salamanca is after premium finishes, fine materials and a [design that blends the classic elegance](/servicios/interiorismo-madrid) of the building with contemporary comforts: home automation, discreet climate control and designer kitchens.

### Luxury renovation trends for 2026

**1. Open-plan kitchens with an island**

The kitchen becomes the social heart of the home, with natural marble worktops, integrated high-end appliances and concealed extraction systems.

**2. Spa-style bathrooms**

Walk-in showers with a rainfall effect, freestanding bathtubs, natural marbles (Calacatta, Statuario), recessed taps and underfloor heating.

**3. Whole-home automation**

Control of lighting, climate, blinds and security from a single app. Integration with voice systems and programmable scenes.

**4. Natural materials**

A predominance of natural stone, fine woods (oak, walnut), brass and glass. A move away from plastic and synthetic finishes.

**5. Architectural lighting**

Professional lighting design with layers of light: ambient, functional and accent. LED integrated into cornicing and recesses.
