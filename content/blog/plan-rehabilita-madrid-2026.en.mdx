## Plan Rehabilita Madrid 2026: an opportunity to refurbish your building

The **Plan Rehabilita Madrid 2026** (Madrid Refurbishment Plan) opens a significant window for residents' associations looking to undertake the refurbishment of their building, renovate the facade or improve energy efficiency with the backing of public funds. With a budget earmarked for refurbishment and an application timetable already in place, it pays to prepare well in advance: the call for applications has a closing date, and the technical paperwork takes time.

In this guide we set out the essentials of the plan, the deadlines and how to approach the application with confidence.

## What the Plan Rehabilita Madrid 2026 is

This is a grant scheme designed to drive the **refurbishment of residential buildings** in the city of Madrid. Its aim is twofold: to improve the state of repair of the existing building stock and to enhance its energy performance, in line with the decarbonisation guidelines for the building sector.

### Key facts about the call for applications

| Item | 2026 reference |
|----------|-----------------|
| Plan budget | Around 50 million euros |
| Applications open | 3 June 2026 |
| Applications close | 30 September 2026 |
| Indicative amount | Up to around 8.000 - 9.000 € per home |
| Application route | Residents' associations |

> The figures above are provided as a market and call-for-applications reference. The definitive conditions, the eligible percentages and the specific requirements are set out in the official terms published by the competent authority, so we recommend checking them before submitting your application.

### The type of works it targets

The plan is intended chiefly for works on the building as a whole rather than on individual homes. The works that typically fit this kind of scheme include:

- Refurbishment and improvement of **facades** and common elements of the thermal envelope
- **Energy efficiency** works (thermal insulation, improvement of the thermal envelope, replacement of building services)
- Renovation of roofs and elements that protect the building against damp
- Improvements linked to structural conservation identified in the building's assessment

## Deadlines: why 30 September 2026 matters

The timetable is one of the most sensitive aspects of any grant scheme. For the Plan Rehabilita Madrid 2026, the application window runs from **3 June to 30 September 2026**.

### Get ahead of the closing date

Although it may look like a generous margin, the reality for a residents' association is that gathering the paperwork takes weeks:

1. **Residents' association resolution** at a general meeting to approve the works and how they will be funded.
2. **Technical assessment** of the building, including the status of the Inspección Técnica de Edificios (ITE — mandatory technical building inspection).
3. **Technical design** drawn up by a qualified team, with measurements and a costing.
4. **Administrative paperwork** (Land Registry and cadastral data, identification of owners, certificates).

That is why we recommend starting the process as early as possible and not leaving it to the final weeks before **30 September**. An application put together in a rush is more likely to contain errors or omissions that delay its processing.

## The ITE and the building assessment

The **Inspección Técnica de Edificios (ITE — mandatory technical building inspection)** is a central piece of any refurbishment. This document, governed at municipal and regional level, certifies the state of repair of the property and is usually a required reference in refurbishment grant schemes.

### The role it plays in the grant

- It helps to **identify the defects** that justify the works.
- It serves to **prioritise** the most urgent works (structure, facade, building services).
- It provides the **technical evidence** that the authorities usually require in order to award the grant.

If the building's ITE is outstanding or has defects on record, tackling the refurbishment under the plan allows two issues to be resolved at once: meeting the obligation to keep the building in good repair and taking advantage of the financial support available. ## Applying through the residents' association

A defining feature of the Plan Rehabilita Madrid 2026 is that the **application is channelled through the residents' association**. This has practical implications worth knowing.

### The usual steps in the procedure

| Stage | Who is involved |
|------|------------------|
| Resolution at general meeting | Residents' association |
| Technical assessment and ITE | Technical team |
| Drafting the design | Architect / quantity surveyor |
| Submitting the application | Residents' association (with technical support) |
| Carrying out the works | Refurbishment contractor |
| Justification and certification | Technical team + residents' association |

### Points to watch

- **Majorities and resolutions:** decisions on works and funding must be taken in accordance with the Ley de Propiedad Horizontal (the Commonhold Property Act).
- **Apportioning the cost:** it is important that each owner clearly understands their share and the effect of the grant on their contribution.
- **Coordinating the paperwork:** centralising the information (Land Registry and cadastral data, owners, certificates) avoids delays in the final stretch of the deadline.

## E-E-A-T: official sources and rigour

The starting information for any grant-backed refurbishment should always be checked against the official sources. In the case of Madrid, the usual references are:

- The **Ayuntamiento de Madrid** (Madrid City Council), responsible for the ITE and much of the municipal planning paperwork.
- The **Boletín Oficial de la Comunidad de Madrid (BOCM)** (the Region of Madrid's official gazette), where calls for applications and regulatory terms at regional level are published.
- The **Agencia Tributaria (AEAT)** (Spanish Tax Agency), for the tax treatment of grants and any deductions associated with energy-efficiency improvement works.

Checking the published terms, the exact deadlines and the eligible percentages against these sources is the surest way to avoid surprises. The amounts and dates given in this guide are indicative and should be confirmed against the current call for applications.
