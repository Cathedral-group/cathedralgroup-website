## What the Passivhaus standard is

The Passivhaus standard (or "passive house") is an international certification for buildings with very low energy consumption, born in Germany in the early nineties. Its premise is at once simple and demanding: to design the building so that it maintains a stable, comfortable indoor temperature throughout the year, keeping the input from active heating or cooling systems to an absolute minimum.

It is not about adding technology, but about getting the envelope right — façades, roof, floors, windows — down to the last detail. The result is a home that "works" in favour of the people living in it: an even temperature, no draughts, a constant supply of fresh air and energy consumption that, according to the standard itself, can cut heating demand by up to 90 % compared with conventional construction.

In the context of a [full renovation in Madrid](/servicios/reformas-integrales-madrid), Passivhaus stops being a label and becomes a project methodology: a way of approaching the works from the standpoint of efficiency, comfort and durability.

In the high-end segment, value is no longer measured by fine materials or bespoke finishes alone. ## The 5 principles of the passive house

The standard rests on five principles that work together. None functions in isolation: the strength of the system lies in their combination.

### 1. Continuous thermal insulation

The building envelope is insulated generously and, above all, without interruptions. Façades, roof and floors in contact with the outside are given a treatment that limits heat loss in winter and heat gain in summer. In Madrid, with its demanding summers and pronounced winters, this principle is decisive for comfort.

### 2. No thermal bridges

A thermal bridge is the kind of point where heat "escapes" easily: where the floor slab meets the façade, the surrounds of windows, blind boxes. Passivhaus design identifies them and resolves them one by one. Eliminating them not only saves energy, it also prevents condensation and the appearance of damp and mould.

### 3. Airtightness

The envelope is sealed so that there is no uncontrolled air infiltration. This does not mean a "closed in" or stuffy home — quite the opposite — but rather that air comes in and goes out only where the design decides, in a controlled way. Airtightness is verified by means of a specific pressure test (the Blower Door test).

### 4. Ventilation with heat recovery

This is one of the most distinctive pillars. A mechanical ventilation system renews the air throughout the home continuously and, by means of a heat recovery unit, makes use of the temperature of the air being expelled to temper the fresh air coming in. The result: air that is always renewed and filtered, without opening windows and without wasting energy.

### 5. High-performance windows and joinery

Windows are the most sensitive point of any envelope. A passive house uses high-performance joinery, normally with double or triple glazing and a thermal break, oriented and sized to suit the position of the sun. In Madrid, good solar protection is just as important as insulation.

| Principle | Main function | Perceived benefit |
|-----------|-------------------|---------------------|
| Continuous insulation | Reduce heat loss and gain | Stable temperature all year round |
| No thermal bridges | Eliminate leaks and condensation | Less damp, lower consumption |
| Airtightness | Control air infiltration | Comfort and efficiency |
| Ventilation with recovery | Renew the air by reusing its heat | Clean air without losing energy |
| High-performance windows | Insulate and manage solar radiation | Acoustic and thermal comfort |

## Applying Passivhaus in a renovation, not only in a new build

There is a widely held idea that a passive house can only be achieved by building from scratch. That is not the case. Refurbishment to Passivhaus criteria — sometimes referred to under the specific standard for renovation — makes it possible to approach those levels of efficiency in existing homes, even in buildings of heritage value.

In Madrid, where much of the high-end residential stock is located in properties of a certain age, this approach is especially relevant. The key lies in a sound initial diagnosis and in integrating the five strategies within the scope of the renovation.

### What is assessed before starting

An [architecture in Madrid](/servicios/arquitectura-madrid) project geared towards efficiency always begins with a rigorous analysis of the home:

- The condition and make-up of the existing envelope (façades, roof, party walls).
- The existing joinery and its thermal and acoustic behaviour.
- Orientation, solar exposure and the solar protection available.
- The technical feasibility of installing ventilation with heat recovery.
- The building's constraints: the residents' association, regulations and, where applicable, heritage protection.

### Fitting in with the regulations

Any intervention of this kind is carried out within the regulatory framework in force: the Technical Building Code (CTE) on efficiency and health and safety, the Madrid City Council ordinances regarding licences and, where appropriate, the regional regulations published in the BOCM. It is worth remembering that certain works require a licence or prior notification, and that protected buildings have specific constraints. A full study takes care of fitting the project into that framework from day one.

## Energy savings, comfort and asset value

The advantages of a passive home are felt on three levels which, moreover, reinforce one another.

### Energy savings

The best-known argument. According to the standard, a passive house can cut heating demand by up to 90 % compared with conventional construction. Lower demand means smaller equipment, more contained bills and a home far less exposed to the volatility of energy prices.

### Comfort you notice every day

- **An even temperature**, with no cold spots next to windows or walls.
- **Air that is always renewed and filtered**, beneficial for people sensitive to allergies.
- **Acoustic comfort**: the insulation and high-performance joinery noticeably reduce outside noise, an enormous asset in Madrid's urban areas.
- **No draughts** and none of the "cold wall" sensation so common in winter.

### Asset value

An efficient, certified home starts at an advantage in the market. The energy rating is increasingly relevant in the decision to buy or rent, and a property with a low running cost and excellent comfort positions itself naturally in the upper segment. Efficiency, in short, has gone from being an extra to being a component of value.
