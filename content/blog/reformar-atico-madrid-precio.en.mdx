## Renovating a penthouse in Madrid: an investment with its own particularities

Penthouses are among the most coveted property types in Madrid. Natural light, private terraces, city views and a sense of exclusivity that few homes can offer. Renovating a penthouse, however, involves a number of technical and regulatory particularities that set it substantially apart from a conventional renovation on an intermediate floor.

In this guide we take an in-depth look at the real costs of renovating a penthouse in Madrid in 2026, the factors that make a project more or less expensive, the neighbourhoods where these renovations are most in demand, and the key decisions that will make the difference between an exceptional result and a mediocre one.

## Particular challenges of renovating a penthouse

### Waterproofing: the critical work item

Waterproofing is, without doubt, the most sensitive aspect of a penthouse renovation. A failure on this item can cause leaks that affect not only your home but the floors below, with all the financial and legal consequences that entails.

The systems most widely used in Madrid for high-end penthouses are:

- **Self-protected bituminous membrane**: between 35 and 55 €/m². A traditional solution with good durability (15-20 years). It requires specialist labour and is the most common option in period buildings.
- **Sprayed polyurethane membrane**: between 45 and 70 €/m². Excellent adhesion, seamless, ideal for complex geometries. Service life of 20-25 years with proper maintenance.
- **Two-layer system with EPDM membrane**: between 60 and 90 €/m². Maximum durability (30+ years), resistant to UV rays and extreme temperature swings. This is the solution we recommend at Cathedral Group for premium projects.

Over the waterproofing comes the terrace screed which, depending on the chosen finish (porcelain stoneware, technical decking, microcement), adds a further 60 to 180 €/m².

### Structure and roof loading

Penthouses present structural limitations that must be assessed before any intervention:

- **Permissible terrace load**: roof slabs tend to bear less load than those on a typical floor. Before designing in heavy planters, hot tubs or thick flooring, a structural survey is essential (between 800 and 2.000 €).
- **Slab reinforcement**: where a need for reinforcement is identified, the cost ranges between 120 and 250 €/m² depending on the system used (carbon fibre, steel sections, concrete overlay).
- **Creating mezzanines or double-height spaces**: in penthouses with sufficient headroom (typically beneath a pitched roof), usable spaces can be created. The average cost is in the region of 400-700 €/m² of floor area gained.

### Thermal insulation: long-term returns

Penthouses suffer greater thermal exposure as they are in direct contact with the roof. Poor insulation translates into high energy bills and reduced comfort, especially during Madrid's summers, when temperatures readily exceed 40 °C.

The most common solutions include:

- **External insulation (external thermal insulation on the roof)**: 60-100 €/m². It does not eat into usable interior floor area and is the most technically efficient solution.
- **Internal insulation (dry lining)**: 40-70 €/m². More economical but slightly reduces the habitable floor area.
- **Injecting existing cavities**: 15-30 €/m². Only viable where an accessible air cavity exists.

The investment in quality insulation typically pays for itself within 4-6 years through savings on climate control, as well as substantially improving the property's energy rating.

## Prices by Madrid neighbourhood

The location of the penthouse has a significant bearing on the final cost, both because of the demands of the market and because of the constructional characteristics of each area.

### Salamanca and Recoletos

Penthouses in the [Salamanca](/zonas/reformas-salamanca) neighbourhood are found in grand period buildings between 80 and 130 years old. Renovations here call for:

- Respect for protected façades (BIC heritage listing or partial listing).
- Fine materials: marble, solid wood, aluminium joinery with thermal break in a finish similar to the original.
- Average price: **2.200 - 3.800 €/m²**.

### Chamberí

[Chamberí](/zonas/reformas-chamberi) combines buildings from the early 20th century with constructions from the 1960s and 1970s. The penthouses here offer excellent value for money for a high-end renovation:

- Generous ceiling heights that allow for bold designs.
- Residents' associations generally well disposed towards quality renovations.
- Average price: **1.800 - 3.200 €/m²**.

### Chamartín and El Viso

An area dominated by buildings from the 1950s to 1980s, many with spacious penthouses and generous terraces. The ideal profile for full renovations that make the most of the terrace as an outdoor living space.

- Average price: **1.600 - 2.800 €/m²**.

### Retiro and Jerónimos

Penthouses with views over Retiro park are among the most highly prized in Madrid. Renovations here tend to be premium or high-end.

- Average price: **2.000 - 3.500 €/m²**.

## Licences and permits specific to penthouses

Works on penthouses require permits that are not always necessary for renovations of intermediate flats:

- **Prior notification**: for interior renovations that do not affect the structure or the façade. Cost: 200-600 € in fees.
- **Minor works licence**: where the terrace is altered, the thermal envelope is modified, or elements visible from the outside are installed. Cost: 600-2.500 € in fees + technical project.
- **Major works licence**: where there is structural modification, a significant change to the layout, or an increase in built floor area. Cost: 2.000-6.000 € in fees + a project stamped by the COAM (Madrid Association of Architects).

In addition, you must obtain **authorisation from the residents' association** for any work affecting communal elements (roof, façade, structure).

## Common mistakes when renovating a penthouse

### Not carrying out a waterproofing survey beforehand

The most costly mistake. Building the terrace without a detailed survey of the condition of the existing waterproofing and of the drainage system can mean having to redo the entire terrace months after the works are completed.

### Underestimating thermal insulation

A penthouse with inadequate insulation can have temperature differences of up to 8-10 °C compared with the outside in summer, pushing the climate control to its limit and generating astronomical energy consumption.

### Ignoring residents' association rules

Many residents' associations have restrictions on the use of terraces, working hours, materials visible from the outside, or the installation of items such as awnings and pergolas. Checking the bylaws before designing saves disputes and costly modifications.

### Failing to plan the site logistics

Hoisting materials up to a penthouse comes at a cost. Façade hoists (mandatory in many cases) cost between 2.000 and 4.000 € a month to hire. Not including this in the initial quote is a common mistake.
