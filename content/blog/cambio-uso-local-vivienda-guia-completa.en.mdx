## Commercial to residential conversion: everything you need to know

[Converting commercial premises to residential use](/servicios/cambio-uso-local-vivienda-madrid) is one of the most profitable property transactions in Madrid, particularly in central neighbourhoods where the price per square metre of commercial premises can be considerably lower than that of a home.

### Planning requirements in Madrid

For a commercial unit to be converted into a home, it must meet the requirements of Madrid's General Urban Development Plan (PGOUM, Plan General de Ordenación Urbana de Madrid):

**Mandatory minimum requirements:**

- Minimum usable floor area: 38 m² (studio) or 25 m² per person
- Minimum clear ceiling height: 2.50 metres
- Natural ventilation and light: every habitable room must have windows to the outside
- Accessibility: access from the building's communal areas
- Not located in a basement or semi-basement
- Compliance with the Technical Building Code (CTE, Código Técnico de la Edificación)

### Step-by-step process

**1. Feasibility study** (2-4 weeks)

Before investing, it is essential to verify that the premises meet the requirements. An architect reviews the planning regulations for the plot, the condition of the premises and the possible layouts.

**2. Technical project** (4-8 weeks)

A registered architect draws up the change-of-use project, which includes drawings, a construction specification, a services study and a compliance assessment.

**3. Planning permission** (3-6 months)

The [change-of-use permit](/blog/licencia-obra-madrid-guia) is applied for from Madrid City Council. This is the longest stage and can vary from one district to another.

**4. Carrying out the works** (3-6 months)

Once permission has been granted, the full renovation of the space is carried out: layout, services and finishes.

**5. First occupation licence** (1-3 months)

Once the works are complete, the certificate of habitability is applied for and the change of use is registered with the Land Registry.

**A real example:** An 80 m² commercial unit in Chamberí can be converted into a high-end home with a total investment of 180.000 - 300.000 €, producing a property whose market value exceeds 450.000 - 650.000 €.

### Common mistakes to avoid

1. **Failing to check the regulations** before buying the premises
2. **Ignoring the residents' association**: some by-laws prohibit changes of use
3. **Not bringing in a specialist architect** from the outset
4. **Underestimating the timescales** of the administrative process
5. **Overlooking the services**: many commercial units lack proper soil pipes or ventilation
