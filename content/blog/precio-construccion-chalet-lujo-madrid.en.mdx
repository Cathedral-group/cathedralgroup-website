## How much does it cost to build a high-end villa in Madrid?

Building a bespoke detached home is one of the most significant investment decisions you can make, and the cost depends on far more variables than the simple price per square metre. According to 2026 market indices, the build cost for a detached house in the north-west of Madrid typically falls within a range of **1.200 to 2.200 €/m²**, while high-end specifications start at **2.000 €/m² and frequently exceed 2.500 €/m²**. It is worth clarifying from the outset that these figures relate solely to **construction**: they do not include the cost of the land, which in enclaves such as La Moraleja or Puerta de Hierro can represent the single largest line in the project.

In this guide we break down the trades, the factors that move the budget and the realistic timelines, so you can plan your [new-build project in Madrid](/servicios/obra-nueva-madrid) with confidence.

### Price per m² by quality level

| Quality level | Build cost (€/m²) | Reference (400 m² detached house) |
|------------------|------------------------------|----------------------------|
| Standard construction | 1.200 - 1.500 €/m² | 480.000 - 600.000 € |
| Mid-to-high quality | 1.500 - 2.000 €/m² | 600.000 - 800.000 € |
| High-end | 2.000 - 2.500 €/m² | 800.000 - 1.000.000 € |
| One-of-a-kind high-end | 2.500+ €/m² | 1.000.000+ € |

These figures are indicative and refer to build cost excluding land. The specific range for each project depends on the geometry of the plot, the brief and the degree of personalisation.

## Cost breakdown of a bespoke detached house

Understanding how the budget is distributed helps you make better decisions about where to invest and where to economise. Broadly speaking, these are the main trades involved in a high-end detached home.

### Foundations and structure

This is the skeleton of the house and usually accounts for between 20% and 25% of the build cost. It includes the geotechnical survey, the foundations (pad footings, raft or piles depending on the ground) and the structure, which in high-end detached houses combines reinforced concrete with composite solutions to span large openings and achieve open-plan spaces. A site with a steep slope or a high water table adds considerably to the cost of this phase.

### External envelope and roof

This covers the building envelope: façades, thermal and acoustic insulation, high-performance external joinery and the roof. A large part of the home's energy efficiency is determined here. The requirements of the **Código Técnico de la Edificación (CTE)** set the minimums, but at the high-end level it is common to exceed them comfortably in order to achieve top energy ratings, which translates into greater comfort and resale value.

### Building services

Plumbing, drainage, electrical installation in line with the **REBT**, climate control (increasingly delivered through aerothermal systems and underfloor heating and cooling), ventilation, solar installations and security systems. In a high-end home, building services are a growing line item, as fully integrated home automation, access control, technical lighting and wellness features (spa, sauna, room-by-room climate control) multiply the complexity.

### Interior finishes

This is the most visible trade and where the step up in quality is most apparent: natural stone cladding, hardwood or large-format porcelain flooring, bespoke joinery, designer kitchens, bathrooms fitted with high-end taps and architectural lighting solutions. The difference between a mid-range finish and a one-of-a-kind one can double the cost of this phase on its own.

### Grounds, swimming pool and plot landscaping

In a detached house, the grounds are an essential part of the project. This trade includes gardening and landscaping, external paving, lighting, plot boundaries, irrigation systems and, very often, a **swimming pool**. A built pool with a concrete shell, heating and a high-end finish is a significant investment in its own right, to which elements such as pergolas, porches, chill-out areas or sports courts are added.

## The factors that most influence the price

Two detached houses of the same size can have very different costs. These are the factors that most tip the balance.

- **Location and characteristics of the plot.** Building in [La Moraleja](/zonas/reformas-la-moraleja), [Pozuelo de Alarcón](/zonas/reformas-pozuelo) or Aravaca means large plots, demanding planning regulations and a standard of quality in keeping with the surroundings. The topography, machinery access and ground conditions have a direct bearing on the foundations and earthworks.
- **Standard of finishes and degree of personalisation.** A fully bespoke project, with one-of-a-kind materials and unique solutions, sits at the top of the range. Standardising construction solutions makes it possible to optimise costs without compromising on excellence.
- **Home automation and smart systems.** The integration of home automation, zoned climate control, energy management, security and entertainment is one of the most significant additions in a high-end home.
- **Architectural design and distinctiveness.** Large spans, cantilevers, double-height spaces, habitable basements or indoor pools call for technical solutions that raise the budget, but also the character and value of the home.
- **Energy efficiency and sustainability.** Opting for a high-performance envelope, aerothermal systems and solar self-consumption increases the initial investment and reduces running costs over the long term.

### What the build cost does not include

When planning the total budget, it is wise to set aside line items that fall outside the build cost per m²:

1. **The land**, which in the north-west of Madrid is usually the most substantial investment.
2. **Professional fees**: architectural design, site management, construction management and health and safety coordination.
3. **Licences, fees and taxes** at municipal level (building licence from the **Ayuntamiento de Madrid** or the relevant municipality) and ICIO.
4. **Utility connections** for the various supplies (water, electricity, drainage, gas).
5. **A contingency allowance**, recommended at between 10% and 15% of the budget.

## Construction timelines for a detached house

Building a high-end detached home is a process that takes time and planning. As a general guide:

- **Preliminary phase (design and licences):** drawing up the design, obtaining professional approval and securing the building licence can take several months, depending on the complexity and the municipality's administrative timescales. The regional regulations published in the **BOCM** and local planning policy shape the timings.
- **Construction work:** once under way, building a quality detached house usually takes between twelve and twenty-four months, depending on the floor area, the distinctiveness of the design and the level of finishes.

Realistic planning from the outset, with a team that brings together design and execution, is the best guarantee of meeting timelines and budget without surprises.

## How to optimise the investment without compromising on excellence

Building with judgement does not mean spending more, but investing better. These principles help to balance ambition with budget:

- Define the brief clearly before you begin; changes during construction are the main source of cost overruns.
- Invest in what cannot easily be changed later — structure, envelope, building services — and keep flexibility for the finishes.
- Commit to energy efficiency: it adds to the cost of the build but reduces running costs for decades.
- Rely on a single point of contact to manage design and execution, so that every decision is taken with the whole picture in view.
