## How much does a full renovation cost in Madrid in 2026?

The cost of a [full renovation in Madrid](/servicios/reformas-integrales-madrid) varies considerably depending on the area, the quality of the materials and the scope of the project. In 2026, the indicative ranges are:

### Prices per m² by level of finish

| Level | Price per m² | Example (100 m² flat) |
|-------|--------------|----------------------|
| Standard | 800 - 1.200 €/m² | 80.000 - 120.000 € |
| High | 1.200 - 1.800 €/m² | 120.000 - 180.000 € |
| Premium | 1.800 - 3.000 €/m² | 180.000 - 300.000 € |
| High-end | 3.000+ €/m² | 300.000+ € |

### The factors that most affect the price

**1. Area of Madrid**

Premium neighbourhoods such as [Salamanca](/zonas/reformas-salamanca), Chamberí or El Viso carry higher costs owing to the complexity of their period buildings, the specific planning regulations and the demand for superior finishes.

**2. Prior condition of the property**

A property that needs all its services replaced (plumbing, electrics, climate control) involves an increase of 30-40% compared with a renovation that covers finishes alone.

**3. Materials chosen**

The difference between standard and premium materials can double or triple the budget. Natural marble, bespoke joinery and home automation are the main drivers of cost.

**4. Architectural design**

A project with bespoke interior design includes 3D renders, material selection and site management, which adds 10-15% to the total budget but guarantees a coherent result. If you are unsure about the scope, take a look at our guide on [full vs partial renovation](/blog/reforma-integral-vs-parcial).

### What does a full renovation include?

A complete full renovation should include:

- Demolition and removal of rubble
- New layout in line with the project
- Complete electrical installation (compliant with REBT)
- Plumbing and drainage
- Climate control (air-source heat pump or split system)
- Flooring and wall coverings
- Internal and external joinery
- Painting and finishes
- Lighting
- Fully fitted kitchen and bathrooms

### Tips for optimising your budget

1. **Set clear priorities** before you start: where should you invest more and where can you save?
2. **Request at least 3 detailed quotes** and compare them line by line
3. **Set aside an extra 10-15%** for unforeseen costs (especially in older buildings)
4. **Don't skimp on services**: plumbing and electrics are the foundation of everything
5. **Choose a full-service practice** that manages both design and execution to avoid cost overruns
