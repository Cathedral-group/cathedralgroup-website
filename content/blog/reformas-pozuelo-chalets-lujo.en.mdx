## Luxury Villa Renovations in Pozuelo de Alarcón: where Madrid lives large

Pozuelo de Alarcón is, in its own right, one of the most exclusive municipalities in the Region of Madrid. With the highest per capita income in Spain, privileged natural surroundings and a housing stock dominated by large detached homes, Pozuelo is home to some of the most valuable residential properties in the country.

Many of these detached houses, however, were built during the 1980s and 1990s, to standards of design, energy efficiency and layout that no longer meet the needs or expectations of today's owners. A full renovation of a detached house in Pozuelo is not merely an aesthetic update: it is the chance to completely reinvent the experience of living in a high-end villa on Madrid's doorstep.

### Why Pozuelo is the premium location par excellence

**Proximity to Madrid**

Less than 15 minutes from Madrid's financial centre via the A-6 and with excellent commuter rail and light-rail connections, Pozuelo combines the calm of a residential area with the connectivity of the capital. Neighbourhoods such as La Finca, Monteclaro, Somosaguas and Montealina offer green, private surroundings without giving up urban amenities.

**Quality of life**

Pozuelo offers a density of green spaces well above the Madrid average, top-tier international schools (American School in Pozuelo, Colegio Estudio nearby in Aravaca), elite sports facilities (the Club de Campo Villa de Madrid nearby) and a level of public safety that ranks among the best in the region.

**Property types**

Pozuelo's housing stock ranges from terraced houses in well-established developments to detached villas of more than 1,000 m² built on plots of 2,000–5,000 m². This diversity allows for renovation projects of widely varying scale and ambition.

**A robust property market**

Sale prices in Pozuelo's premium developments remain stable or are rising, which guarantees that investment in a quality renovation translates into a proportional or greater increase in the property's value.

### Landmark developments in Pozuelo

Each area of Pozuelo has its own planning characteristics, building types and regulations that are worth knowing before planning a renovation.

**La Finca**

Without doubt the most exclusive development in Pozuelo and one of the most prestigious in Spain. Large plots with homes designed by renowned architects, 24-hour private security and its own golf course. Renovations in La Finca must respect the development's strict aesthetic codes, which govern façade materials, heights, colours and fencing.

**Monteclaro**

A well-established development of detached houses from the 1980s and 1990s on plots of 1,000–2,500 m². Many of these homes need a full update of services, insulation and layout. The predominantly south-facing orientation and the generous plot sizes offer enormous potential for ambitious renovation projects.

**Somosaguas**

A residential area with a mix of detached homes and small high-end apartment blocks. The detached houses of Somosaguas are characterised by their mature gardens with large, well-grown trees and private swimming pools. Renovations here must sensitively integrate the renewed architecture with the existing landscaping.

**Montealina**

A gated development with private security, high-end homes and an exclusive community atmosphere. The plots are generous and allow for significant extensions within the permitted building allowance.

**Prado de Somosaguas and Prado Largo**

Areas with mid-to-high-quality terraced and semi-detached houses. Renovations here tend to focus on interior modernisation, energy improvements and the creation of more usable outdoor spaces.

### Types of renovation in Pozuelo's detached houses

**Full renovation with complete reconfiguration**

The most ambitious renovation and the one that delivers the greatest transformation. It involves:

- Complete interior demolition, retaining only the load-bearing structure and the building envelope.
- A new layout tailored to contemporary lifestyles: open-plan spaces, suites with a dressing room and bathroom, work-from-home areas, cinema rooms or a gym.
- Total renovation of services: electrics, plumbing, climate control, home automation.
- New high-end finishes throughout every space.

This type of renovation, applied to a detached house of 300–500 m², can range from 450.000 to 1.500.000 € depending on the finishes selected. For a guide to prices per square metre, see our [full renovation price guide for Madrid](/blog/precio-reforma-integral-madrid-2026).

**Extension and renovation**

Many of Pozuelo's detached houses have unused building allowance still available, which makes it possible to:

- Extend the ground floor to create a large, open daytime living area.
- Build a habitable basement (wine cellar, gym, cinema, enlarged garage).
- Add an extra storey if the regulations permit.
- Build a porch or bioclimatic pergola to extend the living space.

Extensions require a major works licence and a stamped technical project, and must comply with the plot's planning parameters (site coverage, building allowance, setbacks).

**Building envelope and energy efficiency renovation**

Many detached houses from the 1980s and 1990s lack adequate thermal insulation, have aluminium frames without a thermal break and outdated heating systems. An envelope renovation includes:

- External (SATE) or internal thermal insulation.
- Replacement of frames with windows offering high thermal and acoustic performance.
- Installation of an air-source heat pump as the main climate control system.
- A ventilated roof with reinforced insulation.
- Installation of photovoltaic solar panels (Pozuelo's detached houses are ideal thanks to the available roof area).

**Renovation of outdoor spaces**

In a detached house, the outdoors is just as important as the indoors. The most sought-after works include:

- Garden design with professional landscaping.
- Construction or renovation of a swimming pool.
- Barbecue areas and outdoor kitchens.
- Garden and façade lighting.
- Bioclimatic pergolas and glazed porches.

### Indoor–outdoor integration: the essence of life in a detached house

The greatest advantage of a detached house over a flat is its relationship with the outdoor space. A high-end renovation in Pozuelo should maximise this connection:

**Large expanses of glass**

Aluminium frames with a thermal break or high-performance PVC, with large-format sliding panels (up to 6 metres wide) that blur the boundary between the living room and the garden. Systems such as folding doors or lift-and-slide units allow an entire façade to be opened completely.

**Continuous indoor–outdoor flooring**

Using the same material (or visually compatible materials) indoors and on the terrace creates an extraordinary sense of space. Large-format porcelain with a non-slip exterior finish is the most practical solution.

**Porches and bioclimatic pergolas**

Pergolas with adjustable louvres make it possible to create a covered outdoor space that can be used for most of the year. With glass side enclosures, they become a bright, sheltered winter lounge. Brands such as Biossun, Renson and KE offer high-end solutions with home-automation integration.

**The swimming pool as an architectural feature**

The swimming pool ceases to be a simple basin of water and becomes a feature integrated into the garden design and the architecture of the house. Infinity-edge pools, designer pools with a perimeter overflow, chlorine-free natural pools and pools with a movable floor that converts into a terrace are some of the options available.

**Outdoor kitchen**

A firmly established trend in the premium segment: a fully equipped outdoor kitchen with a built-in barbecue, wood-fired oven, griddle, sink, fridge and bar. The materials must withstand the elements: stainless steel, natural stone, porcelain and tropical hardwood.

### Materials and finishes for high-end detached houses

The materials used in a detached house have specific requirements compared with a home in an apartment block:

**Façades**

- Natural stone (limestone, sandstone, granite) for a noble, durable appearance.
- Single-coat render with textured finishes in neutral tones.
- Treated timber cladding (cedar, accoya) for warm accents.
- Ventilated façade systems with ceramic or composite panels for maximum efficiency.

**Roofs**

- Flat or curved ceramic tiles depending on the architectural style.
- Walk-on flat roofs with ceramic paving or timber decking.
- A green roof for detached houses of contemporary architecture.
- Zinc or copper for designer roofs with distinctive geometries.

**External joinery**

- High-performance aluminium with a thermal break (brands such as Schüco, Technal, Cortizo).
- Timber-aluminium: noble timber inside and protective aluminium outside.
- High-end PVC for maximum thermal and acoustic insulation.

**Outdoor flooring**

- Natural stone (granite, quartzite, slate) for maximum durability.
- 2 cm exterior porcelain with a non-slip finish.
- Tropical hardwood decking (ipe, teak) or composite for pool areas.
- Stamped or polished concrete for circulation areas and the garage.

### Services specific to detached houses

**Climate control**

Detached houses have a far larger envelope surface than a flat, which calls for powerful, efficient climate control systems:

- **Air-source heat pump:** The most efficient and sustainable system for detached houses. It provides heating, cooling and hot water with a COP of 4–5 (for every kW consumed, it produces 4–5 kW of thermal energy).
- **Underfloor heating and cooling:** Ideal for detached houses thanks to the large available surface area. Combined with an air-source heat pump, it offers maximum comfort with minimum consumption.
- **Geothermal:** For large plots, geothermal energy is the most efficient option in the long term, although it requires a significant initial investment (boreholes 100–150 metres deep).

**Solar energy**

The roof of a detached house offers an ideal surface for installing solar panels:

- **Photovoltaic:** An installation of 8–15 kWp can cover between 60% and 80% of a detached house's annual electricity consumption. Indicative cost: 10.000–20.000 € without storage batteries.
- **Thermal:** Solar thermal panels to support domestic hot water and pool heating.

**Whole-home automation**

A high-end detached house is the ideal setting for a complete home-automation installation:

- Control of lighting, climate control, blinds, irrigation and security from a single platform.
- IP video-entry systems with remote access.
- Perimeter security cameras with intrusion detection.
- Automated pool control (temperature, pH, chlorination).
- Smart irrigation with humidity sensors and a weather station.

### Licences and permits for detached house renovations in Pozuelo

Pozuelo de Alarcón Town Council has its own planning regulations, different from those of the city of Madrid:

- **Pozuelo General Urban Development Plan:** Defines the building parameters for each area (building allowance, site coverage, heights, setbacks).
- **Major works licence:** Required for structural renovations, extensions and façade alterations. Requires a stamped technical project.
- **Minor works licence / responsible declaration:** For interior renovations with no structural impact.
- **Pool licence:** Building or renovating a swimming pool requires a specific licence with a project covering the filtration system, safety fencing and compliance with setbacks.
- **Development regulations:** Many gated developments (La Finca, Montealina) have additional internal regulations governing materials, colours, fence heights and working hours.

### The renovation process for a detached house: realistic timeframes

**Design and project phase:** 2–4 months. Includes a survey of the current condition, architectural and interior design, material selection and the preparation of a detailed quote.

**Licensing phase:** 2–4 months. Processing the works licence with Pozuelo Town Council and obtaining development permits where applicable.

**Construction phase:** 6–14 months. Variable according to scope: a complete interior renovation can take 6–8 months, whereas a renovation involving an extension and outdoor works can run up to 14 months.

**Snagging and handover phase:** 1–2 months. Final adjustments, professional cleaning, commissioning of services and documented handover.

In total, a full renovation project for a high-end detached house in Pozuelo should be planned with a horizon of 12 to 24 months from the first meeting to the handover of keys.

A detached house built in 1995 with a compartmentalised layout, dated finishes and a lack of insulation becomes, following a full renovation, a bright villa of contemporary lines with large windows, open-plan spaces, an infinity pool and a garden designed by a professional landscaper.
