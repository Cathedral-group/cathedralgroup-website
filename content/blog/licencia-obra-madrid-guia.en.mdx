## Building permits in Madrid: what every property owner needs to know

One of the aspects that causes the most uncertainty when planning a renovation in Madrid is that of building permits. The planning regulations of the Ayuntamiento de Madrid (Madrid City Council) distinguish between several types of authorisation depending on the scope of the works, and not being clear about the difference can mean delays of months, financial penalties and even the suspension of the works.

In this guide we break down everything you need to know in order to apply correctly for the permits for your renovation in 2026: what type of licence you need, how much it costs, how long it takes, what documents you need to prepare and what happens if you fail to comply with the regulations.

## The three types of planning authorisation in Madrid

The regulatory framework currently in force in Madrid establishes three levels of authorisation for works, governed primarily by the Madrid City Council's Ordinance 6/2022 on Planning Licences and Self-Certified Declarations (Ordenanza 6/2022 de Licencias y Declaraciones Responsables Urbanísticas) and the General Urban Development Plan (Plan General de Ordenación Urbana, PGOU):

### 1. Declaración Responsable (self-certified works declaration)

This is the simplest and quickest procedure. It applies to minor works that do not alter the structure, the volumetric form or the architectural configuration of the building.

**Works that require a self-certified declaration:**

- Replacement of interior flooring and wall finishes.
- Interior and exterior painting (without changing the original colour on a listed façade).
- Replacement of sanitaryware and taps without altering the position of soil pipes.
- Changing interior doors.
- One-off repairs to existing installations (electrical, plumbing) without a new connection.
- Installation of air conditioning (with restrictions depending on the district and type of building).

**Features of the procedure:**

| Aspect | Detail |
|---------|---------|
| Processing time | Takes effect immediately upon submission |
| Cost in municipal fees | According to the planning fees ordinance (varies by procedure) |
| Technical project required | No |
| Site management required | No |
| Validity | 6 months from notification |

The self-certified declaration allows the works to begin from the moment it is submitted to the municipal register, which makes it the most efficient procedure. However, this does not mean that the Council cannot carry out an inspection and, if it finds that the works exceed what was declared, order their suspension and open penalty proceedings.

### 2. Minor Works Licence (Declaración Responsable — self-certified declaration)

This is required for more substantial interventions that do not affect the structure of the building but do alter significant elements.

**Works that require a minor works licence:**

- Renovation of bathrooms and kitchens involving changes to the water supply and drainage layout.
- Opening or closing up openings in non-structural partition walls.
- Modification of the interior layout (without affecting load-bearing walls).
- Work on terraces and roofs (waterproofing, paving, installation of pergolas).
- Complete replacement of the electrical or plumbing installation.
- Replacement of exterior joinery.
- Installation of a lift (where no structural alteration is required).

**Features of the procedure:**

| Aspect | Detail |
|---------|---------|
| Processing time | 1 - 3 months (negative administrative silence) |
| Cost in municipal fees | Planning fee + ICIO (3.75% of the PEM) |
| Technical project required | Yes (technical report or outline project) |
| Site management required | Yes (quantity surveyor or technical architect) |
| Validity | 12 months from grant |

The Tax on Construction, Installations and Works (Impuesto sobre Construcciones, Instalaciones y Obras, ICIO) is a municipal levy charged on carrying out any construction, installation or works. In Madrid the rate is 3.75% of the real and actual cost of the works (since 1 January 2024, Tax Ordinance 7/2023).

### 3. Major Works Licence

This is the most comprehensive authorisation and is required for larger-scale works that affect the structure, the volumetric form or the architectural configuration of the building.

**Works that require a major works licence:**

- Full renovation involving structural modification (opening up load-bearing walls, reinforcement of floor slabs).
- Extension of the built floor area.
- Change of use of the property (commercial unit to home, office to residential).
- Works on listed buildings with heritage protection.
- New build construction.
- Full restoration of façades on listed buildings.
- Excavation or earthworks.

**Features of the procedure:**

| Aspect | Detail |
|---------|---------|
| Processing time | 3 - 6 months (up to 12 for listed buildings) |
| Cost in municipal fees | Planning fee + ICIO (3.75% of the PEM) + planning licence fee |
| Technical project required | Yes (outline and detailed project certified by the COAM) |
| Site management required | Yes (chartered architect + quantity surveyor) |
| Validity | 24 months from grant (extendable) |

## Total cost of permits: a practical calculation

To understand the real cost of the process, let us look at an example with a material execution budget (PEM) of €150,000 for a [full renovation in Madrid](/servicios/reformas-integrales-madrid):

| Item | Calculation | Amount |
|----------|---------|---------|
| Municipal fee | 0.4% x €150,000 | €600 |
| ICIO | 3.75% x €150,000 | €5,625 |
| Technical project (quantity surveyor) | Depending on complexity | €2,000 - €4,000 |
| **Estimated total** | | **€8,225 - €10,225** |

### Major works licence

| Item | Calculation | Amount |
|----------|---------|---------|
| Municipal fee | 0.4% x €150,000 | €600 |
| ICIO | 3.75% x €150,000 | €5,625 |
| Planning licence fee | Variable depending on district | €300 - €800 |
| Outline + detailed project (architect) | 6-8% of the PEM | €9,000 - €12,000 |
| Site management (quantity surveyor) | 3-4% of the PEM | €4,500 - €6,000 |
| COAM certification | According to fee scale | €500 - €1,200 |

| Safety coordinator | 1-2% of the PEM | €1,500 - €3,000 |
| **Estimated total** | | **€22,825 - €31,225** |

As can be seen, the process can represent between 6% and 21% of the total budget for the works, depending on the complexity of the procedure required. It is essential to include these costs in the financial planning of the project.

## Required documentation, step by step

### For a self-certified declaration

1. **Self-certified declaration form** completed (available on the electronic portal of the Ayuntamiento de Madrid).
2. **Description of the works** to be carried out, indicating the area affected.
3. **Material execution budget** broken down by item.
4. **Photographs** of the current state of the property.
5. **Proof of ownership** (extract from the Land Registry or title deed).
6. **Public liability insurance** of the company carrying out the works.

### For a minor works licence

All of the above, plus:

7. **Technical report or outline project** signed by a competent technician.
8. **Drawings of the current and proposed state** (plan, sections and affected elevations).
9. **Detailed budget by section** with measurements.
10. **Appointment of site management** (document of acceptance by the supervising technician).
11. **Basic health and safety study** or risk assessment.
12. **Proof of payment** of municipal fees and ICIO.

### For a major works licence

All of the above, plus:

13. **Outline and detailed project** certified by the Official College of Architects of Madrid (Colegio Oficial de Arquitectos de Madrid, COAM).
14. **Full health and safety study**.
15. **Appointment of a health and safety coordinator**.
16. **Construction and demolition waste management study**.
17. **Energy performance certificate** for the current state (where applicable).
18. **Building Evaluation Report** (Informe de Evaluación del Edificio, IEE) if the building is more than 50 years old. In Madrid, the Technical Building Inspection (Inspección Técnica de Edificios, ITE) is also mandatory at 30 years and is repeated every 10.
19. **Authorisation from the residents' association** (minutes of the general meeting) if communal elements are affected.

## Penalties for not having a building permit

Carrying out works without the corresponding licence constitutes a planning offence that can lead to serious consequences:

### Types of offence and penalties

| Severity | Circumstance | Financial penalty |
|----------|----------|-------------------|
| Minor | Minor works without a self-certified declaration | €600 - €30,000 |
| Serious | Works without a minor works licence where one was required | €30,001 - €600,000 |
| Very serious | Works without a major works licence, impact on protected elements | €600,001 - €3,000,000 |

### Additional consequences

In addition to the financial penalty, the authorities may order:

- **Immediate suspension** of the works under way.
- **Reinstatement order**: an obligation to return the property to its original state at the offender's expense.
- **Inability to obtain the first occupancy licence** if the works are not legalised.
- **Problems with the sale or mortgage** of the property, since unlegalised works create discrepancies with the cadastre and the registry.
- **Liability towards third parties**: if damage is caused to neighbours or communal elements, the lack of a licence significantly worsens the owner's position.

## Step-by-step procedure for obtaining your licence

### Step 1: Preliminary technical assessment (1-2 weeks)

Before starting any procedure, it is essential to determine what type of authorisation your project requires. A qualified technician will visit the property, assess the scope of the planned works and determine the applicable administrative procedure.

### Step 2: Drafting the project or technical report (2-6 weeks)

Depending on the type of licence required, the following will be prepared:
- **Descriptive report**: for a self-certified declaration.
- **Outline technical project**: for a minor works licence.
- **Outline and detailed project**: for a major works licence, which must subsequently be certified by the COAM.

### Step 3: Gathering supporting documentation (1-2 weeks)

Land Registry extract, residents' association minutes where applicable, energy certificate, waste management study, etc.

### Step 4: Online submission (1 day)

Since 2020, most planning procedures in Madrid have been handled through the Council's electronic portal. Submission requires a digital certificate or the Cl@ve system.

### Step 5: Settlement of fees and ICIO (1-2 weeks)

Once the application has been submitted, the Council issues the fee assessment. The ICIO can be settled provisionally and regularised on completion of the works.

### Step 6: Resolution and notification (variable)

- Self-certified declaration: immediate effect.
- Minor works licence: negative administrative silence (deemed refusal) after 1-3 months.
- Major works licence: express resolution within 3-6 months; administrative silence is negative (deemed refusal) for major works and changes of use since Law 1/2020.

### Step 7: Notification of commencement of works

Once the licence has been obtained, the start date of the works and the details of the construction company and the site management must be notified to the Council.

## Special cases you should be aware of

### Listed buildings

Madrid has an extensive catalogue of listed buildings. If your property is one of them, the renovation may require:

- A prior report from the Local Historic Heritage Commission.
- A justification report on compatibility with the protected values.
- Specific materials and construction techniques.
- Significantly longer processing times (up to 12 months).

### Change of use (commercial unit to home)

Converting a commercial unit into a home is a complex process that requires a major works licence and must meet all the habitability conditions of the Technical Building Code (Código Técnico de la Edificación) and the Planning Regulations of the Madrid PGOU.

### Works on communal elements

Any intervention affecting the structure, façade, roof or general installations of the building requires prior authorisation from the residents' association, generally by a three-fifths majority of the owners and participation shares.
