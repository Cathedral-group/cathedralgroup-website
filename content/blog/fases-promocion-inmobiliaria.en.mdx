## How does a new-build property development work?

Developing high-end new builds in Madrid is an orderly process in which each phase shapes the next. Understanding the complete cycle helps explain why a project takes as long as it does, where the risks are concentrated and what role a fully integrated developer plays in seeing it through from buying the land to handing over the keys. On [Cathedral Group's new-build developments](/developments) we work through the six phases as a continuous chain, with no gaps and no intermediaries to dilute accountability.

Broadly speaking, a development moves through these stages:

1. **Feasibility study and land acquisition**
2. **Architectural design and permits**
3. **Structuring the financing**
4. **Construction and site management**
5. **Sales**
6. **Handover and after-sales service**

We'll go through each one below.

## Phase 1: feasibility study and land acquisition

Every development begins long before the first crane arrives. The **feasibility study** is the analysis that decides whether a plot is worthwhile, and it is probably the phase where most is won or lost.

### How you decide whether a plot is profitable

The feasibility study brings together three sets of information:

- **Planning:** buildable area, permitted use, heights and setbacks under Madrid City Council's planning rules. A plot is only worth what it allows you to build.
- **Costs:** the price of the land, estimated construction cost, professional fees, permits, charges and taxes.
- **Revenue:** the expected sale price per m² of finished home, compared against equivalent product in the same area.

The sector's golden rule is simple: the **sale value** of the finished product must cover the land, the construction and all the associated costs, leaving a margin that compensates for the risk and the capital tied up over time. As a market benchmark, the cost of land in a high-end development tends to account for a substantial share of the total, and the usual industry ratios place its weight in the following indicative range:

| Item | Indicative share of the total |
|---------|-------------------------------|
| Land | 25 - 40 % |
| Construction (build cost) | 40 - 55 % |
| Professional fees, permits and charges | 8 - 15 % |
| Financing and sales costs | 5 - 12 % |

> These ranges are a general guide for the sector and vary according to location, property type and the market cycle. Every plot calls for its own study.

### Due diligence before buying

Before signing the purchase of the land, the registry status, charges, planning situation and any potential encumbrances are all verified. Acquiring a plot with a hidden problem can jeopardise the entire operation, which is why legal and technical due diligence is non-negotiable.

## Phase 2: architectural design and permits

With the land secured, the design stage begins. The **architectural design** defines the development: layout, number of homes, finishes, energy efficiency and the character of the product.

### From outline scheme to permit

The process usually follows these steps:

1. **Outline scheme:** the initial idea, which sets out the massing and the brief.
2. **Outline design:** the document submitted to apply for the **building permit** from Madrid City Council.
3. **Detailed design:** the full technical detail used to build.

Securing the permit is one of the critical milestones in the schedule. Administrative timescales depend on the complexity of the project and the council's workload, so it pays to allow prudent margins and not commit to handover dates until it has been granted. This phase also defines the **finishes**, which make all the difference in high-end product: bespoke joinery, fine materials, home automation, acoustic insulation and a demanding energy envelope.

## Phase 3: structuring the financing

A development typically combines three sources of funds: the developer's **own capital**, **bank financing** (the classic developer loan) and, where applicable, the **advance payments from buyers** who purchase off-plan.

### The developer loan and investment

The developer loan is drawn down against certifications, that is, as the works progress, and usually requires a percentage of committed sales before it can be fully drawn. The balance between own capital and debt is a strategic decision that determines the final return. For investors interested in taking part in this kind of operation, we develop it within [Cathedral Group's property investment](/capital) division, where we structure the entry of capital with rigour and transparency.

Buyers' advance payments must be protected by a guarantee or insurance policy and held in a dedicated account, in line with the rules on protecting amounts paid on account. It is a safeguard for the buyer and a mark of the developer's integrity.

## Phase 4: construction and site management

With the permit and the financing in place, **construction** gets under way. It is the most visible phase, but its success depends on everything decided beforehand.

### Who's who on site

- **Contractor:** physically carries out the works.
- **Project supervision team:** the architect and quantity surveyor who check that the works are built in accordance with the design and the regulations.
- **Health and safety coordinator:** ensures compliance with safety requirements.
- **Developer:** coordinates the whole, controlling timescales, costs and quality.

### Managing risk on site

The main risks at this phase are **schedule overruns**, **cost overruns** and **quality control**. They are managed with a realistic programme, monitoring by certifications, a contingency for the unexpected and demanding site management. In high-end new builds, where the quality of the finish is the selling point, control allows no shortcuts. ## Phase 5: sales

Selling does not wait until the works are finished. With new builds it is common to start selling **off-plan**, during construction, which brings in liquidity and reduces the sales risk.

### Selling during construction

Selling early offers advantages to both the developer and the buyer:

- **For the developer:** it secures sales before completion, eases the financing and improves cash flow.
- **For the buyer:** access to the product on more favourable terms and the chance to personalise the finishes.

Buying before the building exists has its own safeguards and precautions, which we explain in detail in our guide to [buying off-plan in Madrid](/blog/comprar-sobre-plano-madrid). The sale and purchase contract, the protection of advance payments and transparency over finishes and timescales are the pillars of a well-run operation.

## Phase 6: handover and after-sales service

The development culminates in **handover**, but the relationship does not end there.

### The completion milestones

- **Final works certificate**, signed by the project supervision team.
- **First occupation permit**, granted by the council.
- **New-build declaration and horizontal division** before a notary, with registration at the Land Registry.
- **Handover of the keys** and the homeowner's manual to the buyer.

### After-sales service and warranties

Following handover, the **after-sales service** deals with snags and remedial work. Building regulations set out staggered warranty periods, with broader cover for structural elements and specific periods for finishes and installations. A fully integrated developer stands behind its product after the sale, because reputation is built in the after-sales service too.
